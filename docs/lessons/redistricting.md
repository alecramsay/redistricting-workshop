title: What is redistricting?
---

Redistricting happens at 3 different levels: congress, state legislatures, and local governments.
We will focus on congressional redistricting.

Congressional redistricting is a high-stakes process, because it determines control of the House of Representatives:

* We have two relatively evenly balanced major parties.
* How a state's districts are drawn strongly influences the resulting partisan split (in the extreme via gerrymandering).
* State legislatures frequently draw or control the redistricting process 
  (see [State-by-state redistricting procedures](https://ballotpedia.org/State-by-state_redistricting_procedures){:target="_blank"}).
* So control of state houses influences control of Congress (see [REDMAP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/REDMAP){:target="_blank"} and
  [Ratf**ked: Why Your Vote Doesn't Count](https://wwnorton.com/books/9781631493218){:target="_blank"}).

These contribute to the complex game dynamics of redistricting.
