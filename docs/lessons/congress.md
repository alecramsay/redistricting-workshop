## Misconceptions

There are many misconceptions about how we elect congressional representatives:

* SMD are required by the Constitution -- The Constitution does not mention SMD. 
  Moreover, *Wesberry* clearly implies they aren’t required.
* SMD ensure one-person, one vote (OPOV) -- Before OPOV, states intentionally malapportioned districts to dilute voters.
* We have always used SMD -- Until 1842, smaller states tended to prefer at-large seats. There was no mandate from 1929–1967.
  Congress mandated SMD in the 1967 Uniform Congressional District Act. Congress could change this, e.g., [Fair Representation Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_Representation_Act_(United_States)).
* Congress mandated SMD on principle -- The mandates were largely political.
* There aren’t any alternatives to SMD -- Most major democracies use other systems.
* The size of Congress is fixed in the Constitution -- The size is set by law, the last time in 1929. Congress could change this.

## Apportionment & Representation

Congressional representatives are allocated to states, based on their population in the decennial census.
Total population is used, as opposed to voting-age population (VAP) or citizen voting-age population (CVAP).

That method is set by statute, currently the [Reapportionment Act of 1929](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reapportionment_Act_of_1929){:target="_blank"}. Congress could change this method.

Similar to the well-understood differences in representational power of Senators by state, 
there are also differences in the [power of congressional representatives by state](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1T9vMcV96rWMXj45EfBxhKZyvXd3QCSr1/view?usp=drive_link){:target="_blank"}.

## Legal Requirements ("Constraints")

Maps must be:

* Complete – implicit
* Contiguous – sometime specified (23 of 43 states), never defined re: water
  (see "operational contiguity" in ["Contiguity"](https://medium.com/dra-2020/contiguity-20f23ea15969))
* Free of holes (no donut-hole districts) – generally accepted, but ...
* Equal population (PL94-171) – from OPOV case law
  - Total Population, not VAP or CVAP – expected “innovation” here this cycle
  - 'Roughly' threshold – congressional (~0.75%) vs. state legislative (~10%) (see ['Roughly Equal Population'](https://medium.com/dra-2020/roughly-equal-population-db56b7450595))
  - Some states re-allocate prisoners to their prior home addresses
* Protect minorities (VRA Section 2, PL94-171) – very complex

The ambiguity in these "requirements" contributes to the complex game dynamics of redistricting.
